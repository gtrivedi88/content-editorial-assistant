== Training overview

This training guide provides comprehensive instruction on using the Content Editorial Assistant (CEA), an AI-powered writing analysis tool. Upon completion, users will understand how to effectively use AI assistance while maintaining human oversight and content quality.

=== Training objectives

By the end of this training, you will be able to:

. Understand the capabilities and limitations of AI-assisted writing tools
. Apply the human-in-the-loop principle to all AI-generated content
. Use CEA to analyze and improve technical documentation
. Identify and mitigate risks associated with AI-generated content
. Follow best practices for reviewing and accepting AI suggestions

=== Target audience

* Technical writers
* Documentation managers
* Content reviewers
* Anyone using CEA for documentation improvement

=== Estimated training time

* **Self-paced learning**: 45–60 minutes
* **Hands-on exercises**: 30 minutes
* **Total**: Approximately 1.5 hours

== Module 1: Understanding AI-assisted writing

=== 1.1 What is AI-assisted writing?

AI-assisted writing tools use machine learning models to analyze text and provide suggestions for improvement. Unlike traditional spell-checkers, these tools can:

* Analyze style guide compliance across multiple standards
* Suggest sentence restructuring
* Detect context-dependent issues
* Recommend style improvements
* Generate rewrite suggestions

=== 1.2 How CEA uses AI

Content Editorial Assistant employs a hybrid approach — deterministic rules for reliable pattern matching, and LLM-based analysis for context-dependent judgment:

[cols="1,2,1"]
|===
|Feature |How it works |Human review required

|**Deterministic rules (111)**
|Coded pattern matching against IBM Style Guide, Red Hat Supplementary Guide, accessibility, modular docs, and minimalism
|Yes — context may vary

|**LLM granular analysis**
|Per-block AI analysis for context-dependent style issues
|Yes — may have false positives

|**LLM global analysis**
|Full-document review for tone, flow, structure, and consistency
|Yes — suggestions are advisory

|**AI suggestions**
|LLM-powered rewrite suggestions for detected issues
|**Critical** — always verify

|**Quality scoring**
|Penalty-based score (30–100) with severity weights
|Yes — context matters
|===

=== 1.3 AI limitations you must understand

[IMPORTANT]
====
**AI systems have inherent limitations:**

. **Hallucinations**: AI may generate plausible-sounding but incorrect information
. **Context blindness**: AI may not understand your product, organization, or domain
. **Bias**: AI training data may introduce unintended biases
. **Variance**: Same input may produce slightly different outputs across sessions
. **No true understanding**: AI processes patterns, not meaning
====

== Module 2: Human-in-the-loop principle

=== 2.1 What is human-in-the-loop?

Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) is a design principle ensuring humans maintain oversight and final decision authority over AI-generated content.

[WARNING]
====
**ALWAYS REVIEW AI-GENERATED CONTENT PRIOR TO USE**

This is not optional. Every piece of AI-generated content MUST be reviewed by a human before publication or use.
====

=== 2.2 Why human review is essential

[cols="1,2"]
|===
|Risk |Human review mitigates

|**Factual errors**
|Humans verify accuracy against source materials

|**Inappropriate tone**
|Humans ensure content matches organizational voice

|**Missing context**
|Humans add domain-specific knowledge

|**Security/Privacy**
|Humans catch sensitive information exposure

|**Brand compliance**
|Humans ensure adherence to style guides
|===

=== 2.3 The review process

Follow this process for ALL AI-generated content:

. **Read completely** — do not skim AI suggestions
. **Verify facts** — cross-reference with authoritative sources
. **Check context** — ensure it fits your document's purpose
. **Validate tone** — confirm appropriate voice and style
. **Test accuracy** — for procedures, verify steps work
. **Accept/Modify/Reject** — make deliberate decisions

== Module 3: Using Content Editorial Assistant

=== 3.1 Accessing CEA

. Navigate to the CEA application URL
. No login required — sessions are anonymous
. Your data is NOT stored after the session ends

=== 3.2 Input methods

==== Uploading documents

Supported formats:

* PDF (.pdf)
* Microsoft Word (.docx)
* Markdown (.md)
* AsciiDoc (.adoc)
* DITA XML (.dita, .xml)
* Plain Text (.txt)

==== Direct text entry

. Paste or type content into the editor
. Useful for quick analysis of snippets

=== 3.3 Running analysis

. Select the content type (concept, procedure, reference, release notes) or leave as auto-detect
. Click the **Analyze** button
. Watch real-time progress through three phases:
  * Deterministic rules (~0.5–1s)
  * AI-powered block analysis (per-block progress counter)
  * Global document review
. Review the results in the issue panel

=== 3.4 Understanding results

==== Issue categories

Issues are grouped by IBM Style Guide category:

* **Language and Grammar**: Passive voice, verb tense, anthropomorphism, etc.
* **Punctuation**: List punctuation, possessives, plurals
* **Structure and Format**: Heading levels, sentence length, definitions
* **Audience and Medium**: Accessibility, self-referential text, geographic locations

==== Quality score

CEA calculates a penalty-based quality score:

* **90–100**: Excellent
* **75–89**: Good
* **60–74**: Needs Work
* **Below 60**: Poor

=== 3.5 Using AI suggestions

[CAUTION]
====
The suggestion feature generates content using AI. This content requires thorough human review.
====

. Click an issue card to expand it
. Click **Suggest** to request an AI-powered rewrite
. **REVIEW THE OUTPUT CAREFULLY**
. Choose: **Accept** (apply to editor) or dismiss the issue

=== 3.6 Report panel

CEA provides a slide-over report panel with:

* Summary of findings by category
* Issue counts and severity breakdown
* Quality score

== Module 4: Best practices

=== 4.1 Before using AI assistance

*Do:*

* Have a clear goal for your content
* Prepare source materials for fact-checking
* Understand your audience and purpose

*Do not:*

* Enter personal or sensitive information
* Rely solely on AI for accuracy

=== 4.2 During AI-assisted work

*Do:*

* Review each suggestion individually
* Consider context the AI does not have
* Use your domain expertise
* Document significant AI-suggested changes

*Do not:*

* Accept suggestions blindly
* Skip the review step

=== 4.3 After using AI assistance

*Do:*

* Perform final human review of all content
* Verify technical accuracy
* Check for consistency with other documents
* Follow your team's review/approval process

*Do not:*

* Publish without human verification

=== 4.4 Red flags to watch for

Be especially careful when AI suggestions:

* Include specific numbers, dates, or statistics
* Reference external sources or links
* Make claims about product capabilities
* Include code samples or commands
* Contradict your source materials

== Module 5: Privacy and data handling

=== 5.1 What NOT to enter

[IMPORTANT]
====
**Never enter into CEA:**

* Personal Identifiable Information (PII)
* Customer data or account information
* Credentials, passwords, or API keys
* Confidential business information
* Information not intended for AI processing
====

=== 5.2 Data retention policy

* **Session data**: Deleted when you close the browser (TTL-based expiration)
* **Analysis results**: Not stored after session
* **Uploaded documents**: Processed in-memory only
* **AI interactions**: Not used for model training
* **Feedback votes**: Stored in SQLite for rule improvement (no content retained)

=== 5.3 Security considerations

* CEA runs within the corporate network
* Content is processed by approved AI providers only
* No data is shared with external parties
* Session isolation prevents data leakage

== Module 6: Practical exercises

=== Exercise 1: Basic analysis

. Copy a paragraph from existing documentation
. Paste into the CEA editor
. Click **Analyze**
. Review results and identify:
   * How many issues were found?
   * What is the quality score?
   * What is the most common issue type?

=== Exercise 2: Critical review

. Click an issue card and request a suggestion
. Before accepting, answer:
   * Is the suggested text factually accurate?
   * Does it maintain the original meaning?
   * Is the tone appropriate?
   * Would you publish this as-is?

=== Exercise 3: Provide feedback

. Use the thumbs-up/thumbs-down buttons on issue cards
. Rate at least 5 issues to understand the feedback system
. Note: your feedback directly improves CEA's accuracy
